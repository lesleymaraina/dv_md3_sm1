Recap: Categorical vs. Numerical

Categorical Variables (Qualitative)

  • Definition: Descriptors of qualities or categories of data

    • Represent labels, names, or groups

  • Values:

    • Text
    • Numbers [i.e.: patient IDs, zip codes]

  • Utility: Can count frequencies, mode, or proportions

  • Examples:

    • Treatment group: Placebo, Drug A, Drug B

Numerical Variables (Quantitative)

  • Definition: represent quantities that are measurable and numeric

  • Values: Numbers where mathematical operations(addition, subtraction, averaging) make sense

  • Types:

    • Discrete: Countable values

      • Number of hospital visits

    • Continuous: Measurable values that can take on decimals

      • Blood pressure, cholesterol level

  • Utility: allows for a wide range of statistical analyses

  • Examples:

    • Serum glucose level (mg/dL)

Understanding What Numbers Represent

  • Numeric variables quantify magnitude, intensity, or duration of biological phenomena

  • Each number reflects a measurable process or state:

    • Glucose \(\rightarrow\) metabolic control

    • Creatinine \(\rightarrow\) renal filtration

    • Ejection fraction \(\rightarrow\) cardiac performance

  • Biomedical inference begins with understanding what the number means physiologically, not statistically

  • Cognitive emphasis: A number’s interpretability depends on context — unit, scale, reference range

Why Numeric Data Visualization Matters

  • Almost every biomedical dataset contains numeric measures that capture biological, clinical, or operational observations

    • Clinical: vital signs, laboratory values, dosage levels

    • Molecular: gene expression counts, protein concentrations

    • Operational: procedure durations, cost per encounter

Data Visualization: Seeing meaning in numbers

Numeric visualization:

  • Supports scientific reasoning through visual comparison: spotting differences in magnitude, rate, or variability across contexts
  • Promotes transparency and trust by letting stakeholders visually assess data integrity and trends
  • Framing idea: Numeric visualization is visual reasoning with quantities — the foundation for credible biomedical insight

Data Visualization: Seeing meaning in numbers

Numeric visualization:

  • Makes quantities visible — transforming raw numbers into interpretable biomedical meaning
  • Serves as an early diagnostic lens to detect outliers, implausible values, or data entry errors
  • Allows analysts to see structure, scale, and variation directly — long before statistical modeling is considered

Numeric Data in Context

  • Numeric values have context:

    • Clinical thresholds: normal vs abnormal (e.g., glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL)

    • Temporal frame: before vs after intervention

    • Population frame: adult vs pediatric norms

  • Visualize with reference markers or shaded normal ranges when relevant

Numeric Data in Context

  • Annotate with context (mean line, target zone, or standard-of-care threshold)
  • Encourage consistency in units and axis scaling across related visuals
  • Design goal: viewers should immediately know what “normal” looks like

Example: Serum Creatinine Distribution

Concept: numeric data visualization reveals biological patterns and data integrity within a real biomedical measure

Dataset: Serum creatinine values (mg/dL) from 500 patients — a direct numeric indicator of renal filtration

Visualization: Histogram showing the distribution of values across the patient population

Example: Serum Creatinine Distribution

Histogram:

  • Central peak near 0.8–1.0 mg/dL → expected range for normal renal function

  • Right-hand tail extending beyond 2.0 mg/dL → subgroup with possible renal impairment

  • Extreme outliers (>5 mg/dL) → may represent dialysis patients, transcription errors, or unit inconsistencies

Plot interpretation:

  • Even a simple numeric plot surfaces biological substructure (healthy vs. impaired) and data quality signals simultaneously

  • Visualization transforms numbers into an interpretable clinical landscape — far more revealing than a table or summary statistic

Summary

  • Numeric visualization transforms quantities into visible evidence

  • Understand what numbers represent biologically before encoding them visually

  • Maintain context and scale integrity — avoid misleading visuals

  • Visualization is the first step of statistical modeling, not an afterthought
